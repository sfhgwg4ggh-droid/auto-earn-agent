Buying Guide: What to Look For in a USB-C Hub for MacBook Pro

Power Delivery (PD)

Make sure the hub supports at least 60W PD for charging your MacBook Pro. Most we tested offer 100W, which is ideal for the 16-inch model. Lower PD hubs will charge slower or even drain the battery under heavy load.

Video Output

If you have a single external monitor, HDMI 2.0 (4K@60Hz) is sufficient. For dual monitors, look for hubs with both HDMI and DisplayPort (like Anker Elite) or Thunderbolt (CalDigit). Some cheaper hubs only mirror the display.

Data Speeds

For external SSDs, ensure at least USB 3.2 Gen 2 (10Gbps). Thunderbolt 4 hubs offer up to 40Gbps. SD card slots: UHS-II for photographers (200MB/s+).

Build Quality & Heat

Aluminum hubs dissipate heat better. We noticed the Anker and CalDigit stayed cooler than the plastic HyperDrive. Also check cable length – short cables are fine for desk use, but longer ones help with laptop on lap.

Port Selection

Think about your peripherals: Do you need Ethernet? A headphone jack? SD and microSD? Some hubs omit the headphone jack (like CalDigit TS4). Make a list of what you plug in daily.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will a USB-C hub charge my MacBook Pro?

Yes, if the hub supports Power Delivery (PD) passthrough. Look for at least 60W for MacBook Pro 13-inch, and 96-100W for 16-inch. The hubs we recommend all support 100W.

Can I use a USB-C hub with a closed MacBook Pro (clamshell mode)?

Yes, as long as the hub provides power and video output. All hubs in this guide work in clamshell mode when connected to an external monitor and power.

Why does my external monitor flicker when using some hubs?

This is often due to cable bandwidth or poor shielding. We observed flickering with the HyperDrive on some Dell monitors. For stable 4K@60Hz, use a quality HDMI cable and ensure the hub is plugged directly into your MacBook (avoid daisy-chaining). If flickering persists, try a different hub.

Do I need a Thunderbolt hub or a regular USB-C hub?

If you only need one 4K monitor and peripheral speeds up to 10Gbps, a regular USB-C hub (like Anker Elite) is fine. For dual 5K/6K displays, fast external SSDs (NVMe), or daisy-chaining, invest in a Thunderbolt 4 hub (CalDigit TS4).

Can I use a Windows laptop with these MacBook Pro hubs?

Most USB-C hubs are cross-platform, but some features (like video output over DisplayPort) may require drivers. The CalDigit TS4 works perfectly with Windows laptops, as do Anker and Belkin. Check manufacturer specs.
