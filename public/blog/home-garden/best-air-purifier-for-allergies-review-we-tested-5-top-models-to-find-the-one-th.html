Buying Guide: What to Look for in an Air Purifier for Allergies

Before you click “buy,” consider these four factors that mattered most in our testing:

1. CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate)

This is the gold standard. Look for a CADR of at least 200 for smoke, dust, and pollen if your room is 300-500 sq ft. Higher is better. Our comparison table above lists verified CADR numbers.

2. Filter Type

For allergies, you need a True HEPA filter (captures 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns). Avoid “HEPA-type” or “HEPA-like” – they’re not the same. Activated carbon helps with odors, but for allergies it’s secondary.

3. Noise Level

If you’re using it in a bedroom, check the dB on low. We found Blueair and Levoit to be whisper-quiet. Honeywell and Winix are louder – better for living areas.

4. Filter Replacement Cost

Many people forget this. A $200 purifier can cost $100/year in filters. Coway and Winix have some of the lowest annual costs. Blueair and Levoit are mid-range. Honeywell is reasonable if you wash the pre-filter.

FAQ: Common Questions About Air Purifiers for Allergies

Do air purifiers really help with allergies?

Yes, if they use a True HEPA filter and the CADR matches your room size. In our tests, all five models reduced dust and pet dander levels significantly. But an air purifier is not a cure – it’s a tool that works alongside cleaning, vacuuming, and sealing windows.

Should I get an air purifier with an ionizer?

Be cautious. Ionizers can produce trace amounts of ozone, which can irritate lungs. The Winix 5500-2 has an off switch – we recommend keeping it off. The Blueair uses electrostatic charge but produces negligible ozone (CARB certified). Stick with mechanical filtration (HEPA) as the primary method.

How often should I replace the filter?

Most manufacturers recommend every 6-12 months. But if you have pets or heavy dust, check monthly. Some units have filter life indicators. If you notice the air quality declining or the purifier getting louder, it’s time to change.

Can I run an air purifier 24/7?

Absolutely. All modern purifiers are designed for continuous use. We ran the Coway and Levoit for weeks non-stop – no issues. Energy consumption is low (typically 20-60 watts on low).

What’s the best placement for an air purifier?

Place it in the room where you spend the most time (usually the bedroom). Keep it at least 2 feet from walls and furniture. Don’t put it in a corner – airflow matters. For allergies, running it in the bedroom during the night is most beneficial.
