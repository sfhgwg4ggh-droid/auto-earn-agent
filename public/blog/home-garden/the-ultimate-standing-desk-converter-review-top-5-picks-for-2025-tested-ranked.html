Buying Guide: How to Choose the Best Standing Desk Converter

1. Measure Your Desk and Monitors

Before buying, measure your desktop depth (typically 24–30 inches) and width. Most converters are 24–36 inches wide. Also measure your monitor sizes—dual 27-inch monitors need at least 36 inches width. Some converters don't support monitor arms, so check clearance.

2. Consider Weight Capacity

A standard dual monitor setup with a laptop and accessories weighs 20–30 lbs. Choose a converter that can handle at least 35 lbs to give you a margin. If you have a heavy all-in-one, look for crank or electric options with higher capacities (UPLIFT crank holds 50 lbs).

3. Lift Mechanism: Gas Spring vs. Electric vs. Crank

  • Gas Spring: Smooth, quick, requires no power. Best for most users. But gas springs can weaken after a few years.
  • Electric: Effortless, often with memory settings. Quiet but slower. More parts that can fail. Generally more expensive.
  • Crank: Manual, but very reliable and high weight capacity. Takes more effort, but lasts forever. Good for heavy setups.

4. Stability at Full Height

We found that wobble is the #1 complaint for many converters. Wide models (like VariDesk Pro Plus 36) and those with reinforced bases (UPLIFT) are more stable. Avoid cheap narrow models if you type heavily.

5. Cable Management

Look for built-in cable grommets or trays. Without them, your desk becomes a spaghetti disaster when you lift the converter. VariDesk and FlexiSpot have good cable routing.

6. Assembly and Weight

Most converters come partially assembled, but some (like UPLIFT) require more setup. Also consider weight—if you plan to move it often, lighter models (FlexiSpot M2B at 33 lbs) are easier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can a standing desk converter support dual monitors?
Yes, many models like the VariDesk Pro Plus 36 and UPLIFT Desk V2 are wide enough (36+ inches) to hold two 27-inch monitors. Always check the width and weight capacity before buying.
Q: Are gas spring standing desk converters safe?
Absolutely. Gas spring converters use a sealed piston that lifts the platform smoothly. They are tested for thousands of cycles. The spring can weaken over time (5+ years), but it's rare to fail suddenly.
Q: How long does it take to assemble a standing desk converter?
Most gas spring converters take about 10–20 minutes—just attach the upper platform and keyboard tray. Electric or crank models may take up to 45 minutes if you need to mount a crank handle or connect wiring.
Q: Will a standing desk converter fit on my existing desk?
As long as your desk is at least 24 inches deep and has a clear surface wide enough for the converter base (typically 24–36 inches), it will fit. For desks with a center drawer rail, measure clearance—some converters overlap.
Q: What is the difference between a standing desk converter and a full standing desk?
A converter sits on your existing desk and lifts your equipment. A full standing desk replaces the entire desk and usually has adjustable legs. Converters are cheaper and easier to install, but full desks offer more customization and leg clearance.
