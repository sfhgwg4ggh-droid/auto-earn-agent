Cordless Stick Vacuum Buying Guide

Suction Power

Measured in Air Watts (AW). For carpets, aim for at least 150 AW. For hard floors, 100 AW is sufficient. The Dyson V15 and Samsung Jet 90 are the most powerful in our test.

Battery Life

Look for a model that lasts at least 40 minutes on low power. Hot-swappable batteries (like Samsung and LG) give you unlimited runtime. Keep in mind that max power mode cuts battery time by 70–80%.

Weight & Handling

If you have stairs or need to carry the vacuum often, choose something under 6 lbs. The Samsung Jet 90 is the lightest. The Dyson and Shark are a bit heavier but still manageable.

Floor Type Compatibility

Most modern cordless stick vacuums handle all floor types, but check for a brush roll shut-off feature when using on delicate hardwood. The Dyson, Samsung, and Tineco all have this.

Maintenance & Filter Cleaning

All models have washable filters that need cleaning every 1–3 months. The Dyson and LG have the easiest filter access. The Shark has a self-cleaning brush roll – a big plus for pet owners.

Dustbin Capacity

Larger bins (0.8 L+) mean fewer trips to the trash. The Shark Vertex Pro has the biggest bin. The Tineco and LG are smaller but still fine for daily cleaning in a medium home.

Accessories Included

Check what comes in the box. Most include a crevice tool, dusting brush, and combination tool. The Dyson V15 includes a hair screw tool for pet beds. The Samsung Jet 90's telescoping wand is a standout.

FAQs About Cordless Stick Vacuums

Are cordless stick vacuums as powerful as corded?

Not quite. The most powerful cordless models (like Dyson V15) now match many corded uprights for daily cleaning, but for deep cleaning heavy carpets, a corded vacuum still has an edge. For 90% of homes, a top-tier cordless stick vacuum is sufficient.

How long do cordless stick vacuum batteries last?

Varies by model. On low power, expect 30–60 minutes. On max power, 8–15 minutes. Battery degradation occurs after 2–3 years, but many brands sell replacement batteries.

Can I use a cordless stick vacuum on area rugs?

Yes, most have a motorized brush roll suitable for low- to medium-pile rugs. For thick shag rugs, consider setting the brush roll to low or turning it off to avoid tangles.

Is a self-emptying station worth it?

It's a convenience feature. If you have allergies or hate touching dust, it's worth the extra cost. Samsung and LG offer self-emptying models, but they're more expensive and take up more space.

How often should I wash the filters?

Every 1–3 months depending on use. Wash with cold water and let dry completely (24 hours) before reinstalling. Never run the vacuum with a wet filter – it can damage the motor.
