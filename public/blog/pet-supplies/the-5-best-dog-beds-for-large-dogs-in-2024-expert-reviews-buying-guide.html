What Makes a Great Large Dog Bed?

Before diving into our top picks, let's cover the key features you should look for when shopping for the best dog bed for large dogs.

Size & Thickness

Your dog should be able to fully sprawl out with legs extended. For most large breeds, aim for at least 48 x 36 inches. Extra-large dogs (over 100 lbs.) may need 54 x 42 inches or bigger. Thickness matters too—a 4- to 7-inch high-density foam base prevents bottoming out.

Fill Material

Memory foam offers the best orthopedic support, contouring to your dog's joints. High-resilience foam is a good budget-friendly alternative. Avoid low-density polyfill—it will flatten quickly under a heavy dog.

Cover Durability & Cleanability

Look for machine-washable, water-resistant covers with heavy-duty zippers. Nylon, microfiber, or Oxford cloth are common. Waterproof liners are a bonus for accidents or drool.

Support & Orthopedic Design

An orthopedic dog bed for large dogs should have a supportive base and a comfortable top layer. Egg-crate foam, convoluted foam, or gel-infused memory foam can help with pressure relief and temperature regulation.

How to Choose the Best Dog Bed for Large Dogs – Buying Guide

Here are the most important factors to consider when making your final decision.

Measure your dog

Lay your dog down on his side and measure from nose to tail. Add 12–16 inches to get the ideal bed length. Also measure his height when he's sitting to ensure bolsters aren't too tall.

Check the foam density

Memory foam should have a density of at least 3 lbs. per cubic foot for heavy dogs. High-resilience foam (like in Big Barker) can also work well.

Consider your dog's sleeping style

  • Sprawler: Get a flat, rectangular bed with no bolsters or low bolsters.
  • Curler: A donut or bolstered bed works best.
  • Digger: Look for a very durable cover (nylon or heavy-duty Oxford) with a zipper that's not easily destroyed.

Waterproof is a must for some

If your dog drools, has accidents, or is prone to digging wet muddy paws into the bed, choose a bed with a removable waterproof liner or a waterproof cover.

Cleaning convenience

Most covers are machine washable, but check the care instructions. Some foam can't be submerged, so you'll need to spot clean.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size dog bed does a large dog need?

Most large dogs (70–100 lbs.) need a bed at least 48 x 36 inches. Extra-large breeds (100+ lbs.) often need 54 x 42 or larger. Always measure your dog and add room for stretching out.

Is memory foam better for large dogs?

Yes, memory foam provides superior pressure relief and joint support. High-density foam is also good, but avoid cheap polyfill. Memory foam beds last longer under heavy weight.

How do I clean a large dog bed?

Remove the cover and machine wash on gentle cycle with cold water. Air dry or tumble dry on low. For the foam base, spot clean with a damp cloth and mild detergent. Never machine wash foam.

Should I buy a bed with bolsters or without?

Bolsters offer head and neck support and a sense of security. However, some large dogs prefer a flat surface to fully stretch out. If your dog likes to sleep leaning on something, bolsters are great; if he sprawls, go without.
